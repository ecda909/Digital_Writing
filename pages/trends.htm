Trends |

Things that are on trend are always pushing forward.

What are “Trends”?

The word “trend” in this context is the aspects of content that make the content highly spreadable overtime. Trends are usually influenced by the media, current events, and emotional content. These trends are just the observation of what made content popular and spreadable online at the time. A lot of popular articles or posts are about a subject, then that subject is a trend. Trends very in longevity, but always focus on how popular the trending topic is. If we talk about certain formats for memes involving the president (which is currently a popular topic) we can see that content has continually been made about him since he entered media as a politician. The lifespan of content relies on these trends, if the content is outdated or doesn’t appeal to trends then it has a low chance of spreading. Trends appeal to emotions through things such as animals or footage to gain awareness and make people feel the need to share the content with others online, so they feel the same emotional response. An article “What Makes Content Viral?”, by Jonah Burger and Katherine L. Milkman, provide insight into the emotional aspects of content that make content spreadable and trendy.

How Trends are formed?

As previously mentioned, popular content will appeal to emotion and topics that popular content shares are trends. Emotions make content popular and spreadable, but how does this form trends? Trends are formed once a large amount of audience on the internet popularizes content that shares similar traits. For example, if a movie releases and everyone either loved or hated it, then they will share their opinion and feelings online. This large number of users discussing the movie causes the movie to be a trending topic because lots of content is being created on it. Burger and Milkman express that emotion is related to how popular content gets so popular. We associate emotion with given topics and topics that are emotionally relevant will be trends due to their popularization online.

What Content Trends?

As we have discussed emotion fuel topics are what initiates trends, but what emotions cause more popularization and trending topics. Burger and Milkman found that, “results suggest that while external drivers of attention (e.g., being prominently featured) shape what becomes viral, content characteristics are of similar importance”, which shows that content that appeals to emotion is just as, if not more spreadable then promoted content. Their study shows that emotion plays a key role in deciding what trends online. Further research by Burger and Milkman present that the characteristics that effect people emotionally such as awe, anger, positivity, or surprise in content will only add to it popularity and Spreadability between users. Content that appeals to enough users through emotion have higher tendencies of trending than those that don’t appeal to emotion.
