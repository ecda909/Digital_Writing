4Chan?

Yes, 4Chan.

It's Not A Person

4chan is a website, not a person. 4chan is so unknown to the general populace that it is sometimes mistaken for being a person. Many people have heard the name, but don’t know anything about it other than that it’s a website. Somehow, the most mischievous website on the Internet has flown under most people’s radar. 4chan is the primal breeding ground for the dankest, spiciest, and most offensive memes. It is the mitochondria of the memetic cell. It is a website built upon meme culture.

4chan is basically a giant anonymous forum with different subsections categorized upon interest (they call these boards) [1]. People post pictures, ask questions, share files, discuss anime, troll, meme, etc. This type of website is typically known as an imageboard. The thing that’s really different about 4chan is that it advertises all of its deviant content on its homepage. There are plenty of boards on 4chan that aren’t explicit in nature, but there are many trolls and pranksters lurking within 4chan. I wouldn’t want my young kid going on it or think that it’s a good idea to visit it at work.

4chan is notorious for having a high concentration of unethical activities within its boards. Since its users cannot be held accountable for what they post, the ugliest sides of people often manifest themselves. Think about how much bolder you are once you’re driving your car. Now, consider that feeling amplified by the anonymity and freedom from consequences that 4chan offers its users.

What Goes on 4Chan?

4chan has 22 million monthly visitors [3]. Half of them are American [3]. It’s estimated that roughly 70 percent of 4chan’s users are male [3]. Around 1 million posts are made by users on 4chan every day [3]. Currently, the board that gets the most posts a day is the /pol/ board [4]. /pol/ is the Politically Incorrect board. We’ll just say this is where some of the most offensive memes are made. Many users on /pol/ take full advantage of the anonymity of 4chan to proudly exclaim, in a public forum, the things that they are normally hesitant to say in real life. Some of these users seek an oasis in the desert of political correctness, conformity, and manners that society demands of them. But mostly, it’s just a bunch of casual bigotry in the world’s biggest alt-right echo chamber. Right behind /pol/ is /v/, which is the video games board [4]. Not far behind that is the anime board [4]. Hopefully, this gives you an idea of the average user’s priorities.

Here's Where The Memes Come In

As hinted at earlier, many popular memes have originated from 4chan. One exceptional example is greentext stories. On 4chan, placing a ‘>’ symbol at the beginning of a comment line turns the text of the line to green [6]. Usually, greentext is used to tell humorous, personal stories; sometimes they are relatable and show a humorous perspective on the human experience, and others are ridiculous tales of fiction.
