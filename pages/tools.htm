There are a lot of tools for Spreadability and here's what they are.

What are “Tools” of Spreadability?

Tools refers to the creations and inventions that have contributed to simpler means of spreadability. These tools have made it easier for users to share content to one another online and offline. Tools can be as broad as books, since we can share information through them, to the social media’s share button, which was made to spread content. These tools have either made connections between the audience faster or have made it convenient to share content with others. Understand what mechanisms are in place to make the content more spreadable will help audiences understand how content can spread so efficiently.

Impact of Tools

People have been inventing tools that spread content since the creation of language. Some of the most important inventions revolve around the spread of information. Basics like writing and paper have been the first contributors of spreading information because they allowed people to store their words and give them to others rather than just talking directly. The printing press is hailed as one of the most important inventions because it allowed us to create books on a mass scale and provide content and media to a much larger audience in less time. These basics have allowed inventions in modern times to capitalize on how we communicate and share information. Modern times presented the telephones, the internet, e-mail, text messaging, social media, etc. which all contribute to our ability to spread content more freely to each other. We have continually pushed to make content more available and in doing so we create the tools that allow content to have spreadability.

Modern Tool Examples

Reddit provides its users a platform on which they may freely express themselves behind the safety of aliases. Posts and comments are judged by users based on votes; this gives users a means to promote or suppress content on the website. There are many active, popular and niche subreddits; literally anyone could find a subreddit that would interest them. You can still get a lot out of reddit without making an account or participating.

Social Media – platforms that house audiences which share uploaded content and observe content shared by other users.

Text Messaging – allows users to communicated through text or visuals and share content to one another through this text/visual exchange format.

Links/Hyper Links – URLs that can be formatted to bring a user to a given web page by the clicking it.

E-Mail – An online mail system that allows users to send content to one another
