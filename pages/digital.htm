The Digital Age

Now we move into modern day.

Ever since the rise of the desktop PC and the Internet, we have been slowly entering the digital age. Once social media came about that slow crawl turned into a full-on sprint. At this point in time there is virtually nothing that can’t be done from a computer, cell phone, tablet, watch, AI assistant, etc. Just as we have moved into a digital age so has the way that we communicate and share.

In the digital age memes thrive. There are memes about politics, weather events, celebrities and even grapes. Memes which have always been a part of human culture, make up a large part of the content that is being produced for social media. We mention a few of the popular channels that are used to spread memes in the “Channels” section of the site. There are many forms of memes that exist. Traditional memes feature a picture of something with text or some sort of caption. As time progressed so did popular meme formats. Now there are twitter formatted memes, memes that use videos or GIFs, and more. The popularity of memes in the digital age is thanks to the technologies that we have been afforded. Direct messaging, retweeting, liking, texting, and commenting are some of the ways that a meme can gain popularity. Compared to the oral and print age, the spread of something such as memes can reach a much larger audience in such a short period of time.

Example of the Spreadability of a Digital Meme:

In November of 2018, the phrase “They did surgery on a grape” took the internet by storm. In 2010 a video was posted by Edward Hospital showcasing a new surgical system capable of peeling the skin off of a grape. In 2017 Cheddar shared a modified version of the video that had the now popular phrase “They did surgery on a grape.” In November of 2018 a screen shot of the surgery grape video was posted on Instagram and it spread like wild fire from there. Numerous people were creating memes based around the phrase. Some mocked the fact that surgery was done on a grape, having in mind that surgery is mostly done on humans. Others commented on just how far into the future we’ve come. The stickiness of this phenomenon could be broken down according to the six principles of Chip and Dan Heath from their book Made to Stick.

Principle Reasoning
Simplicity This meme was sparked by 6 simple words. Those words have now become a hashtag and punchline for the Internet.
Unexpectedness It’s not everyday that you hear about the medical procedure that our produce are going through. So, hearing a about a grape’s surgery certainly generates interest and curiosity.
Concreteness Anything concrete should be clear and free from any confusion. The same 6 words develop an image in your head, and in the post that went viral there is an actual image of the surgery. It would be hard to misunderstand what this meme is trying to communicate.
Credibility The credibility of the surgery grape comes from the source of the meme and the video accompanied by it.
Emotions Emotions is what led people to make more memes about surgery and grapes. Those that were shocked, compared to grapes to humans in their memes. Others that impressed or aware of the future that TV and movies promised, made jokes about what we actually have in this “present future”.
Stories The phrase “They did surgery on a grape” tells a story that captures people’s attention. The compelling nature of the short sentence is due to many of the principles listed above.
