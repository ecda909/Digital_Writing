Twitter

The place to be when you have a thought.

So What Is Twitter?

Twitter is website and app that allows users to communicate with their followers using a small amount of words at a time. Created in 2006, the app was initially developed as a group text messaging service, by Jack Dorsey and others. Using 140 characters or less, users were encouraged to answer the question “What are you doing?” or “What’s happening”. The small allotted space for commentary paired with pictures and/or videos has impacted the landscape of modern communication and has allowed Twitter to grow into an important social outlet for updates, media, and information.

Audience

The people on Twitter resemble the same demographics of Instagram. The main users are around the 18-34 age range. The social network has roughly 326 million active users, and what they see on their feed comes as a result of the other users that they follow. This audience is familiar with how things spread across Twitter. For example, it is not uncommon for something to gain overnight success and then never really be mentioned again. Since Twitter is driven more by words, compared to picture and video driven platforms such as Instagram and YouTube, users follow the people who they are interested in listening to. So, influencers have a really big presence and an important role on the platform.

Memes

A big theme for everything on Twitter is that it is simple and not dense. Tweets are short spurts of opinions, news, ideas etc. Memes are similar in this way, they usually don’t take long to understand, and they do not require a whole lot of brain power. This is a reason why many memes do well on Twitter and sometimes even are created there. Twitter is also designed as a place to share and spread, and that is mainly because of the retweet feature. A user who sees a tweet that they want to share with their followers has the option to retweet it. They can either retweet the post as is, or they can also add their own commentary in addition to the tweet. So, a lot of times when a funny or meme-esque post is retweeted, the people who see also retweet it and thus the spreading ensues.

Memes On Twitter

On twitter memes are a lot more than pictures or videos with clever captions. Sometimes a tweet itself can be a meme, other times an exchange on twitter could garner meme-worth attention. Traditional picture and caption memes still exist in the Twitter-sphere. They are just used and showcased in a Twitter specific way. For example, there would be a picture going around that people are using in memes. On twitter, simply tweeting something and posting that picture instantly make it a meme in the Twitter space.
